Our World

Wildwoven Hikes exists to reconnect people with the world — not just through landscapes, but through shared stories, guides who know the land by heart, and trails shaped by tradition, wonder, and joy.

We believe that hiking isn’t just exercise — it’s how we remember what it means to be human. That’s why our routes are curated with care: one might lead you to a pop-up drink hut in a mountain pass, another through a silent forest where birds wait for you to listen.

Our hikes aren’t about conquering peaks — they’re about weaving people, places, and time together.

Popular: Lofoten, Norway

Above the Arctic Circle, Lofoten offer some of Europe’s most dramatic and untouched hiking terrain. Towering granite peaks rise straight from the sea, with narrow trails weaving through fjords, fishing villages, and windswept beaches. Hikers traverse ridgelines with panoramic views of turquoise bays and snow-capped summits.

Our Goal

At Wildwoven Hikes, our goal is to create journeys that connect people — not just with landscapes, but with the stories, cultures, and communities that shape them. We believe that meaningful travel goes beyond the trail; it’s about walking with intention, learning with curiosity, and treading lightly.

Every hike we offer is designed to support local guides, preserve natural habitats, and celebrate cultural heritage. Whether it’s sharing a meal in a mountain village or learning the names of native plants, our experiences aim to leave both people and their backpacking pets, a journey to remember.

Community

Every hike is a doorway into the lives and traditions of those who call these landscapes home. We work closely with local communities to ensure our journeys are respectful, enriching, and mutually beneficial. From homestays and market visits to craft workshops and cultural meals, your experience supports real people — not just tourism. These encounters aren’t side-notes; they’re the heart of the trail. Local guides bring depth to the journey, sharing stories that don’t appear on maps and inviting you into places tourists rarely go.

Our mission is not only to explore remote paths, but to strengthen the threads that connect people across cultures. By investing in local partnerships, fair wages, and long-term relationships, we help sustain traditions and livelihoods that might otherwise be overlooked. When you hike with us, you're not just seeing a place — you’re becoming part of its living story.

Eco

We believe adventure should uplift nature, not harm it. That’s why we work with local communities, minimize transport emissions, and prioritize sustainability in every trail we offer.

We hike with guides who live on the land, source regional ingredients (when possible) for every meal, and walk close but not directly on wildlife habitats. Our goal is to ensure that the trails you walk today remain wild, welcoming, and thriving tomorrow.

July: Western Norway

Have you seen the 'zig-zag' landscape of Sunnmøre, Sogn og Fjordane? Well, that's where we're going. Get your boots and raincoats ready because the strong winds in this part of Norway has the rain going sideways. It's an easy and short trail, 'svele with brunost' will be served (imagine a small pancake) and we will be surounded by fjord sceneary on all fronts. Get ready for Oktober's feature.

The weather shifts fast in these parts - bring everything. It's better to have a raincoat and not use it, than not have one and face the rain puring sideways, which Sogn og Fjordane is so charmingly known for.

August: Lofoten, Norway

August doesn’t cool down — it opens up. The cliffs are sharp, the sea is clear, and the light lingers longer than it should. This is the month for salt in your hair, wide skies, and ridge trails that make lunch taste better. Our guides know which coves catch the sun and where to stop when the wind turns poetic. Come walk where the land feels borrowed, and the views feel earned.
