Hikes

Our current hikes span a variety of subjects. We consistently update, to make repeat hikers feel like every trail offers something new — a shift in season, a new guide, or a story they haven’t heard yet.

Lofoten, Norway

Coastal · Varied terrain · Arctic light and ocean air

Craggy cliffs, soft beaches, and light that doesn’t quit. This hike takes you along coastal ridges and down to small coves where the sea feels close enough to touch. Expect changing terrain, bright skies, and a trail that makes even seasoned hikers pause to take it all in. Perfect for those craving salt air, sweeping views, and a route that feels like an unfolding film.

Lantern Lake Trail

Evening hike · Campfire endpoint · All gear provided

Not all hikes start at sunrise — this one waits for twilight. Lantern Lake winds through soft paths as the light fades, ending with stories, drinks, and a fire you didn’t know you needed. It’s slow, quiet, and a little bit magical. Perfect for evening wanderers and anyone who prefers stars to summit views.

Saltfjord Crossing

Coastal · Tidal timing · Local seafood snacks

A hike between land and water — where stillness feels cinematic. Saltfjord Crossing traces the fjord’s edge, with wide views, salt in the air, and the occasional seal sighting. There’s a drink stop near the tide pools and a seafood moment if you’re lucky. Great for coastal moods, evening light, and low-sweat awe.

Northfall Trail

Moderate · Mixed forest · Good for active beginners

This trail climbs gently but steadily — and so does your mood. Skypath Ridge offers sweeping views, guide banter, and a lookout where drinks arrive just as your legs start to wonder why. The terrain is mixed: a bit of rock, a bit of breeze, and a lot of sky. Recommended for curious hikers with a taste for elevation and good company.

Cragstep Loop

Challenging · High elevation · Great for seasoned hikers

Cragstep Loop isn’t technical, but it does demand focus and steady footing. The trail climbs into rocky terrain with long views and sparse cover — bring layers and plenty of water. It’s a favorite among returning hikers who want elevation without ropes or scrambling. Best for confident hikers who enjoy long ridgelines and open sky.
